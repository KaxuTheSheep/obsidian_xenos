## Overview
Lethe, designated [[Procyon B]], is a white dwarf — the dense stellar remnant of a once more massive star. It is the secondary component of the [[Procyon]] system and a powerful source of high-energy radiation, particularly in the ultraviolet. Though vastly smaller and dimmer in visible light than [[Procyon A|Kairos]], Lethe exerts a profound influence on the system’s radiation environment and evolutionary history.

## Physical Properties
- **Designation:** [[Procyon B]]
- **Type:** White dwarf
- **Stellar remnant of:** Former main-sequence star (initially more massive than [[Procyon A|Kairos]])
- **Mass:** ~0.6 solar masses
- **Radius:** ~Earth-sized
- **Surface temperature:** ~7,700–8,000 K
- **Luminosity:** ~0.0005× the Sun (visible), but strong in ultraviolet
- **Spectral colour:** Blue-white, dominated by ultraviolet emission

## Spectrum and Output
Lethe emits relatively little visible light compared to [[Procyon A|Kairos]], but its output is heavily skewed toward shorter wavelengths. Ultraviolet radiation dominates its emission profile, making it the primary UV source in the system. This radiation penetrates space more effectively than visible light and plays a major role in shaping the atmospheric chemistry and surface conditions of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]].

## Stellar History — The Common Envelope Event
Before becoming a white dwarf, Lethe’s progenitor expanded into a red giant, triggering a common envelope event with [[Procyon A|Kairos]]. During this phase, Kairos was engulfed within the outer layers of the expanding star.

This interaction had several lasting consequences:

- **Mass transfer:** [[Procyon A|Kairos]] accreted material, increasing its mass and altering its evolution
- **Orbital shift:** The system lost significant mass, increasing the separation between the two stars
- **Envelope ejection:** The outer layers of the red giant were expelled, leaving behind the dense core — now Lethe

## System Influence

Although faint in visible light, Lethe is a constant and inescapable source of ionizing radiation throughout the [[Procyon]] system. Its ultraviolet output contrasts sharply with the warmer, visible-light dominance of [[Procyon A|Kairos]], creating a dual-radiation environment.

For [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]], this means:

- Persistent exposure to background UV radiation
- Enhanced atmospheric ionization
- A secondary, colder light source that subtly alters perceived stellar colour and shadowing

## The Silent Remnant

Lethe is no longer evolving in any dramatic sense — it is a cooling stellar core, radiating away the residual heat of its past life. Unlike [[Procyon A|Kairos]], whose changes unfold over time, Lethe’s transformation is already complete. What remains is a stable, slowly fading ember — a reminder of a violent past event that reshaped the entire system.
