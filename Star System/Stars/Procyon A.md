## Overview
Kairos, designated [[Procyon A]], is an F-type yellow-white star and the primary energy source for [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]. It is the dominant light source in the [[Procyon]] system, significantly more luminous than Earth's Sun and sitting at the hotter end of the stellar spectrum. From the surface of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] however Kairos appears a warm orange — its yellow-white light contrasted against [[Procyon B|Lethe]]'s cold blue-white ultraviolet output, shifting its perceived colour toward warmer tones in the eyes of any surface observer.

## Physical Properties
- **Designation:** [[Procyon A]]
- **Type:** F-type subgiant
- **Luminosity:** 6.9× the Sun
- **Surface temperature:** ~6,530 K
- **Mass:** ~1.5 solar masses
- **Radius:** ~2.05 solar radii
- **Spectral colour:** Yellow-white — perceived as warm orange from [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]'s surface

## Spectrum and Output
Kairos emits predominantly across the visible spectrum, peaking toward yellow-white. It produces significant ultraviolet output beyond that of the Sun, though this is secondary to [[Procyon B|Lethe]]'s UV contribution. Infrared output is present but not dominant.

## The Ticking Clock
At the moment of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]'s capture Kairos had only just begun its subgiant transition — less than 1 million years into the process. The shift is real but imperceptible on human timescales, manifesting only as a slow drift in luminosity and radiation output over geological time. For life on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] this represents a continuous and accelerating selective pressure — the star that gave the surface world its energy is gradually, inexorably changing. Kairos will eventually mirror what [[Procyon B|Lethe]] already underwent, though on a timescale vast enough that no living thing on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] has ever perceived the change directly.
