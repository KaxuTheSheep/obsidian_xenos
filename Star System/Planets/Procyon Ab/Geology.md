# Erebian Era

## Crust and Core
[[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] possesses an iron-nickel core comprising approximately 60–70% of its total planetary volume — far exceeding Earth's proportionally smaller core. This generates an exceptionally strong magnetic field, partially shielding the subsurface from cosmic radiation during the rogue phase, and drives intense geothermal heat throughout the crust.

The crust averages 30–50km in thickness — thinner proportionally than Earth's but significantly denser, composed primarily of iron-rich basaltic material. High surface gravity of 2.4g compresses this material into a rigid, heavy shell with limited flexibility.

[[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] lacks a true continental/oceanic crustal dichotomy. Instead the surface is dominated by two crustal types — a uniform dense basaltic primary crust covering the majority of the planet, constantly recycled by vigorous tectonic activity, and volcanic highlands where prolonged volcanic accumulation has built thicker, marginally less dense elevated platforms. These highlands represent the closest equivalent to stable landmasses on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]], though they remain geologically active and impermanent on long timescales.

The high iron content throughout gives the crust a dark, metallic character — surfaces range from deep grey to dark bronze depending on oxidation state and mineral composition.

## Volcanic Activity
[[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] is an intensely volcanic world. The combination of a massive iron-nickel core, high geothermal heat flux, and active crustal recycling drives continuous volcanic activity across the planetary surface. During the ante capture period this activity occurs entirely beneath a frozen or near-frozen surface, venting heat and chemistry into the subsurface rather than the open [[atmosphere]].

The dominant volcanic style is shield volcanism — low, broad structures built from highly fluid iron-rich basaltic magma. High gravity and low magma viscosity prevent the construction of tall peaks, instead spreading lava flows across vast horizontal distances. Individual shield structures may span thousands of kilometres, their flanks merging into continuous volcanic plains. These plains represent the majority of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]'s primary crust, constantly being resurfaced by new flows and recycled at tectonic boundaries.

Explosive stratovolcanism occurs but is secondary — limited to regions where volatile-rich pockets accumulate sufficient pressure. These eruptions are violent and localised, contributing significantly to subsurface gas chemistry including hydrogen sulphide, methane, carbon dioxide, and sulphur dioxide.

Serpentinisation operates planet-wide as a continuous abiotic process — iron-rich crustal rock reacting with subsurface water to produce methane. This reaction requires no volcanic activity directly, occurring wherever water contacts the iron-dominated crust, and represents a persistent chemical energy source independent of vent activity.

Hydrothermal vent fields concentrate along tectonic boundaries where crustal recycling is most active. Given the global extent of tectonic activity these fields are effectively planet-wide, creating a distributed network of chemically energetic subsurface environments. It is within these vent fields that both [[Ferrovita]] and [[Sulphovita]] emerge and persist throughout the ante capture period.

The volcanic highlands represent zones of prolonged accumulation — regions where eruption rates have historically exceeded crustal recycling, building thicker elevated platforms above the primary basaltic plain. These highlands are geologically active but comparatively stable, and will become significant in shaping surface terrain following capture.

## Subsurface Hydrology
During the ante capture period [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] maintains a global subsurface liquid water system beneath a thick planetary ice shell. The surface is frozen across its entirety — a consequence of zero stellar energy input during the rogue phase — but geothermal heat flux from the iron-dominated core is sufficient to keep the full volume of subsurface water liquid beneath the insulating ice layer. This configuration mirrors theoretical models of rogue planet habitability and is consistent with analogous systems observed in ice-covered moons within stellar systems.

Water on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] was acquired primarily through asteroid collisions during the ejection event, supplementing whatever water was present at formation. Post-capture, as surface ice melts under Kairos and [[Procyon B|Lethe]]'s combined energy input, this water will constitute approximately 80% surface coverage — vast shallow seas across the flat basaltic primary crust, with volcanic highlands representing the remaining 20% of exposed surface.

Despite being globally connected, the subsurface water system is biologically inert across most of its extent. Chemical energy gradients sufficient to sustain life exist only at two hydrothermal vent cluster zones, both located within the same hemisphere but separated by a significant distance. These two clusters represent the only points of biological activity on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] during the ante capture period — islands of chemistry in an otherwise energetically dead global ocean.

[[Ferrovita]] occupies the deepest, most chemically aggressive zones of these vent clusters. [[Sulphovita]] occupies the cooler peripheral zones of the same clusters. Despite sharing a physical environment the two domains are chemically isolated from one another by thermal barriers and incompatible fluid chemistry, maintaining independent biospheres throughout the rogue phase.

Post-capture hydraulic reconnection — driven by surface melting, pressure redistribution, and fracture propagation through the ice shell — will eventually bring these isolated biospheres into contact. Given the same-hemisphere proximity of the two vent clusters this contact is expected to occur relatively rapidly on geological timescales, representing the first interaction between the two founding domains of Xenian life.

The fluid environment of Pocket B is characterised by moderately acidic, chemically diverse conditions maintained by a combination of volcanic outgassing, serpentinisation, and hydrothermal fluid circulation.

**Solvent**
- Water — dominant solvent, ~85–90% of fluid volume
- Sulphuric acid — dissolved, pH 3–4, concentration ~0.1–1 mmol/L

**Dissolved Gases**
Maintained in solution by high subsurface pressure:
- Carbon dioxide (CO₂) — ~20–30 mmol/L, volcanic waste product
- Hydrogen sulphide (H₂S) — ~15–25 mmol/L, primary biological energy compound
- Methane (CH₄) — ~10–20 mmol/L, secondary biological energy compound
- Hydrogen (H₂) — ~5–10 mmol/L, produced by serpentinisation

**Dissolved Minerals**
- Sulphate (SO₄²⁻) — ~50–100 mmol/L, dominant electron acceptor in biological redox chemistry
- Iron (Fe²⁺) — trace concentrations, significantly lower than [[Geology#Pocket A|Pocket A]]
- Silicates — trace concentrations

**pH and Solvent Character**
Bulk fluid pH sits between 3–4 — moderately acidic, chemically active, and stable enough to support peptide-based biochemistry. The relatively high sulphate concentration is the defining geochemical feature of Pocket B, providing the electron acceptor that underpins [[Sulphovita]]'s sulphur-coupled methane oxidation pathway.
## Terrain
During the ante capture period the surface of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] is defined entirely by ice. A 17km thick planetary ice shell covers the entire surface, forming a featureless frozen expanse across the majority of the planet. Beneath this shell the global subsurface ocean persists, kept liquid by geothermal heat from below.

The ice surface is not entirely uniform. Active tectonics and geothermal heat flux from below create visible surface expressions — long fracture lines, pressure ridges, and scarps where crustal movement has stressed and deformed the ice shell from beneath. Cryovolcanic features are present in geologically active regions, where pressurised subsurface water forces through fractures and refreezes at the surface, building low irregular mounds and flow fields of refrozen slush.

Beneath the ice the primary basaltic crust is flat and largely featureless — vast dark plains of dense iron-rich rock resurfaced continuously by volcanic flows. Tectonic boundaries are the most geologically active zones, marked by vent fields, fracture systems, and localised upwelling of fresh basaltic material.

Volcanic highlands represent the only significant vertical relief on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]. Built from prolonged shield volcanic accumulation, these broad elevated platforms rise above the primary crust but remain submerged beneath the ice shell during the ante capture period. Their upper surfaces sit closer to the ice ceiling than the surrounding plains, creating regions of reduced ice thickness above them — zones that will be among the first to breach the surface following capture.

The overall character of ante capture [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] is one of dark, frozen stillness at the surface — a featureless ice world betraying nothing of the geochemical activity occurring kilometres below.
