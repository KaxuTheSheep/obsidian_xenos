# Erebian Era

## Structure and Pressure
[[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] possesses an extremely dense atmosphere with a total surface pressure of **~47 atm**. This atmosphere is a composite structure formed from two primary sources — a retained primordial envelope and continuous volcanic outgassing — with minor contributions from trace chemical processes.

The high gravity of 2.4g allows Xenos to retain large quantities of light gases, particularly hydrogen and helium, which would be lost on smaller worlds. As a result, the atmosphere remains thick, stratified, and chemically reducing throughout the Erebian Era.

## Primordial Envelope
The majority of the atmosphere — approximately **78% (~36.66 atm)** — originates from the planet’s initial formation and has been retained due to strong gravity and lack of stellar stripping during its rogue phase.

- **Hydrogen — 58% (~27.26 atm)**  
    The dominant atmospheric component. Hydrogen forms a highly reducing background environment and plays a critical role in subsurface chemistry, particularly in hydrothermal systems where it acts as a primary electron donor for [[Ferrovita]] metabolism.
- **Helium — 20% (~9.4 atm)**  
    Chemically inert and largely non-reactive. Helium contributes significantly to total atmospheric pressure but plays no direct role in surface or subsurface chemistry.

This primordial envelope defines the baseline chemical environment of Xenos — reducing, dense, and stable over geological timescales.

## Volcanic Outgassing
Approximately **21% (~9.87 atm)** of the atmosphere is continuously replenished through volcanic activity. These gases originate from the interior and are injected into the atmosphere through persistent geological processes.

- **Carbon dioxide — 8% (~3.76 atm)**  
    A major oxidised carbon reservoir. CO₂ dissolves into the subsurface ocean, contributing to fluid chemistry and acting as a key reactant in [[Novus Ferrovita]] methanogenesis.
- **Methane — 5% (~2.35 atm)**  
    Produced both volcanically and through abiotic serpentinisation. Methane represents a significant reduced carbon source and contributes to subsurface chemical disequilibrium.
- **Sulphur dioxide — 4% (~1.88 atm)**  
    A highly reactive gas that dissolves readily into subsurface water, contributing to sulphur cycling and acidification processes relevant to [[Sulphovita]] environments.
- **Hydrogen sulphide — 2% (~0.94 atm)**  
    A critical energy substrate for [[Sulphovita]], supplying the primary electron donor for hydrogen sulphide oxidation pathways.
- **Water vapour — 1.2% (~0.56 atm)**  
    Present primarily near active volcanic regions. Most water is sequestered in the global ice shell, but vapour contributes to atmospheric and subsurface exchange processes.
- **Carbon monoxide — 0.8% (~0.38 atm)**  
    A partially oxidised carbon species, transient and reactive, contributing to minor redox processes in both atmosphere and subsurface chemistry.

Volcanic outgassing is continuous and planet-wide, maintaining atmospheric composition and supplying the chemical inputs that sustain subsurface ecosystems.

## Trace Gases
The remaining **~1% (~0.47 atm)** consists of minor constituents:

- **Argon**
- **Nitrogen**
- **Phosphine**
- **Ammonia**

These gases are present in low concentrations, originating from volcanic activity and crustal chemistry. While not dominant, they contribute to overall chemical complexity and may play minor roles in niche reactions within the subsurface environment.

## Atmospheric–Ocean Interaction
During the Erebian Era, the atmosphere is physically separated from the subsurface ocean by a **~17 km global ice shell**. Direct interaction between atmosphere and ocean is therefore extremely limited.

Gas exchange occurs indirectly through:

- Volcanic conduits penetrating the ice shell
- Cryovolcanic activity transporting subsurface material upward
- Slow diffusion through fractures and stress zones

As a result, atmospheric composition influences subsurface chemistry only gradually, with hydrothermal systems remaining the dominant drivers of chemical disequilibrium.

## Chemical Character
The atmosphere of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] is fundamentally **reducing and chemically unbalanced**, characterised by the coexistence of:

- Reduced gases (H₂, CH₄, H₂S)
- Oxidised gases (CO₂, SO₂)

This disequilibrium is not resolved abiotically due to physical separation and continuous replenishment from internal processes. Instead, it provides the long-term chemical gradients that underpin the emergence and persistence of both [[Ferrovita]] and [[Sulphovita]].
