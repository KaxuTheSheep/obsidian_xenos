### Overview
Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis is the third and final lineage of [[Ferrovita Firmus]], emerging from [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus]] populations at approximately 400 Million AC through defect variations extending the electrophoretic mechanism from juvenile-only to adult capability. Its defining innovation is not electrophoresis itself — Tactus already has this — but a detachment mechanism allowing an established adult to voluntarily separate from its rock substrate in response to Fe³⁺ depletion and relocate to a new surface. Mobilis is the most resilient Firmus lineage and the direct ancestor of [[Ferrovita Novus]].

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Vent core and marginal zones of [[Hydrology#Pocket A|Pocket A]], including zones of localised Fe³⁺ variability where relocation capability provides meaningful survival advantage
- **Temperature:** ~200–250°C, consistent with [[Ferrovita Firmus]] range
- **Pressure:** Extreme — deepest subsurface zones
- **Chemistry:** Consistent with [[Ferrovita Firmus#Environmental Niche]], tolerates localised Fe³⁺ depletion through relocation rather than dormancy
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Ferrovita Firmus#Environmental Niche]] entirely

### Membrane
Follows [[Ferrovita Firmus#Membrane]] entirely.

The rock-facing pyrite interface in Mobilis carries additional electrochemical capability absent in Rudis and Tactus — the same gradients that normally drive rock integration can be partially reversed under conditions of sustained Fe³⁺ depletion, selectively dissolving the attachment interface sufficiently for fluid dynamics and gravity to complete separation. This reversal is the detachment mechanism — an extension of existing membrane electrochemistry rather than a novel structural addition.

### Electrophoresis and Navigation
Follows [[Ferrovita#Electrophoresis and Navigation]] entirely.

Mobilis extends electrophoresis from juvenile-only to adult capability. Post-detachment adults accumulate electron charge as dissolved Fe³⁺ in Pocket A fluid fails to meet reduction demand, generating passive drift toward the strongest positive electrochemical potential in the vicinity — Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces. Upon contact the rock-facing interface re-establishes through normal pyrite accrual and iron reduction resumes.

Detachment is metabolically costly and represents a last resort rather than routine behaviour — Mobilis detaches only when local Fe³⁺ has depleted to the point where continued attachment provides insufficient energy to maintain gradients. Adult electrophoresis therefore activates only under depletion conditions, not as a continuous navigational state.

### Reproduction
Follows [[Ferrovita#Reproduction]] entirely. Juvenile navigation follows [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus#Electrophoresis and Navigation]] — Mobilis juveniles use the same directed electrophoretic drift as Tactus juveniles, with equivalent survival improvement over Rudis.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Ferrovita Firmus#Dormancy and Death]] entirely.

Dormancy is significantly rarer in Mobilis than in Rudis or Tactus. Where those lineages have no response to local Fe³⁺ depletion other than dormancy or death, Mobilis detaches and relocates. True dormancy in Mobilis occurs only when Fe³⁺ depletion is basin-wide rather than localised — a substantially less common scenario than the localised depletion events that regularly force Rudis and Tactus into dormancy.

### Ecological Character
Mobilis dominates across most Firmus zones by the later Erebian Era. Its relocation capability converts localised Fe³⁺ depletion from a dormancy or death event into a recoverable condition, compounding its resilience advantage over Rudis and Tactus across geological timescales. Rudis and Tactus persist in stable high Fe³⁺ zones where depletion is rare enough that relocation provides insufficient additional advantage to drive competitive exclusion — but wherever chemical variability is a recurring pressure Mobilis's dominance is decisive.

Mobilis's adult mobility makes it the widest-ranging Firmus lineage — the first Ferrovita capable of sustained movement across Pocket A's vent core zones, albeit on geological timescales. This mobility is the direct precursor to the free-floating existence that [[Ferrovita Novus]] will develop from Mobilis populations at the cooler vent margins.

### Evolution
Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis emerges from [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus]] populations at approximately 400 Million AC as defect variation extends the electrophoretic mechanism into adult capability and develops the detachment mechanism. It is the final evolutionary lineage within Ferrovita Firmus — no further divergence occurs within the Firmus domain beyond Mobilis.

At the cooler vent margins, Mobilis populations experiencing chronic Fe³⁺ scarcity accumulate defect variation enabling hydrogen oxidation coupled to CO₂ reduction as a supplementary metabolism. This divergence produces [[Ferrovita Novus]] at approximately 280 Million AC — documented in [[Ferrovita Novus]].
