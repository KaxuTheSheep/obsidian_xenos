### Overview
[[Ferrovita Novus]] Parcus is the founding lineage of [[Ferrovita Novus]], emerging at approximately 280 Million AC from [[Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis]] populations in the cooler vent margins of Pocket A. Parcus is the earliest expression of methanogenesis in [[Ferrovita]] history — a Mobilis-equivalent organism that has developed the capacity to perform methanogenesis but has not yet shifted its metabolic centre of gravity away from iron reduction. Methanogenesis operates exclusively as a transitional metabolism during detachment and transit, extending survival windows between rock surfaces without displacing iron reduction as the primary energy source.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Cooler vent margins of [[Hydrology#Pocket A|Pocket A]], consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus]] range. Favours zones where Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces are present but widely spaced — environments where extended transit survival provides meaningful competitive advantage
- **Temperature:** ~110–130°C, consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus]] range
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus]] range
- **Chemistry:** Consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus#Environmental Niche]]
- **Energy source:** Iron reduction primary at rock surfaces, methanogenesis supplementary during detachment and transit

### Metabolism
Follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Metabolism]] entirely.

Parcus uses methanogenesis exclusively as a transitional metabolism during detachment and transit between rock surfaces. When attached to Fe³⁺-rich rock iron reduction dominates entirely — methanogenesis contributes negligibly when rock-bound Fe³⁺ is abundant. When detached and navigating via electrophoresis methanogenesis sustains reduced but viable metabolic activity, extending the transit survival window significantly beyond what [[Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis]] can achieve on dissolved Fe³⁺ alone.

Pathway switching follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Pathway Switching]] — iron reduction dominates at rock surfaces, methanogenesis activates passively as Fe³⁺ availability drops during transit.

### Electrophoresis and Navigation
Follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Electrophoresis and Navigation]] entirely.

Electrophoresis in Parcus operates primarily during the juvenile phase and post-detachment transit — consistent with the early Novus pattern described in [[Ferrovita Novus#Electrophoresis and Navigation]]. When attached to rock Parcus is as sessile as Firmus lineages. Electrophoretic drift activates upon detachment, navigating toward the next Fe³⁺-rich surface.

### Reproduction
Follows [[Ferrovita#Reproduction]] entirely.

Parcus juveniles benefit from the methanogenesis fallback described in [[Ferrovita Novus#Reproduction]] — if initial rock attachment fails, methanogenesis sustains the juvenile long enough to navigate toward an alternative surface. Juvenile survival rates are meaningfully higher than any Firmus lineage as a result.

### Ecological Character
Parcus occupies cooler vent margin zones where Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces are widely spaced enough that extended transit survival provides meaningful competitive advantage. Its selective advantage over [[Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis]] is most pronounced in these zones — the methanogenesis transit window allowing Parcus to cross distances between suitable attachment sites that Mobilis cannot reach before electrochemical gradients decay. In zones where rock surfaces are densely packed Mobilis and Parcus coexist without strong competitive exclusion. Parcus dominates progressively as rock surface spacing increases toward the cooler vent margins.

Methane waste accumulates locally in Pocket A fluid as a consequence of Parcus methanogenesis during transit. This output is ecologically insignificant at the scale of the broader subsurface water system during the Erebian Era — its ecological significance belongs to the Photian Era.

### Evolution
[[Ferrovita Novus]] Parcus is the founding Novus lineage — the first expression of methanogenesis in [[Ferrovita]] history, emerging at approximately 280 Million AC from marginal [[Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis]] populations under chronic Fe³⁺ scarcity. The divergence was gradual, with no single event marking the transition from Mobilis to Parcus.

Parcus gives rise to [[Ferrovita Novus Liber]] at approximately 150 Million AC as progressive selection in the most Fe³⁺-scarce peripheral zones shifts the metabolic centre of gravity from iron reduction toward methanogenesis until continuous free-floating existence becomes viable. The divergence is documented in [[Ferrovita Novus Liber]].

Parcus itself persists across the remainder of the Erebian Era as the dominant rock-attached Novus lineage — its iron-reduction-primary strategy remaining competitive wherever Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces are accessible, with methanogenesis providing the transit capability that defines its niche advantage over all Firmus lineages.
