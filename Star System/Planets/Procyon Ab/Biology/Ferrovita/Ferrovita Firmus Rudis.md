### Overview
[[Ferrovita Firmus]] Rudis is the founding lineage of [[Ferrovita Firmus]] and the most primitive documented form of life on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] — the earliest form of life on Xenos that can be unambiguously defined as living out of first [[Ferrovita]] organisms that emerged during the early Erebian Era at approximately 800 Million AC. No metabolic innovations, no directed navigation, no adult mobility. Rudis is the reference point against which all subsequent [[Ferrovita]] divergence is measured.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Deepest, most Fe³⁺-rich vent core zones within [[Hydrology#Pocket A|Pocket A]], where rock surfaces are densely packed and juvenile chance-settlement succeeds at viable rates
- **Temperature:** ~200–250°C, consistent with [[Ferrovita Firmus]] range
- **Pressure:** Extreme — deepest subsurface zones
- **Chemistry:** Highest Fe³⁺ concentration in Pocket A, strongest H₂ flux from vent outflow
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Ferrovita Firmus#Environmental Niche]] entirely

### Electrophoresis and Navigation
Electrophoresis is absent or insufficiently developed in Rudis to produce meaningful drift. Juveniles following bud separation drift entirely passively through Pocket A fluid, carried by thermal convection and fluid turbulence rather than any electrochemical gradient. Juvenile mortality is extremely high — most buds settle on unsuitable surfaces or exhaust their residual electrochemical gradients before contacting Fe³⁺-rich rock.

Rudis compensates through continuous bud production over geological timescales — a strategy of quantity over survival efficiency.

### Reproduction
Follows [[Ferrovita#Reproduction]] entirely. No lineage-specific modifications to the budding mechanism. Juvenile survival is governed entirely by passive drift and chance contact with Fe³⁺-rich rock rather than any directed navigation.

### Ecological Character
Rudis occupies the most Fe³⁺-rich vent core zones of Pocket A — the same zones where rock surface density is highest and juvenile chance-settlement succeeds at sufficient rates to sustain populations without directed navigation. Its defining ecological character is persistence through volume — continuous bud production across geological timescales offsetting the extreme juvenile mortality inherent to passive drift.

Rudis persists as a viable lineage throughout the Erebian Era. It does not disappear when Tactus and Mobilis emerge but continues to exist in zones where the selective pressure for electrophoresis is insufficient to drive the innovation — specifically the densest Fe³⁺-rich vent core zones where suitable rock surfaces are abundant enough that chance-settlement remains competitive. It is outcompeted by [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus]] in zones where rock surfaces are more widely spaced and directed juvenile navigation meaningfully improves establishment rates.

### Evolution
[[Ferrovita Firmus]] Rudis has no divergence event of its own — it is the founding lineage, the unmodified baseline. Its evolutionary history is defined by what emerges from it rather than what it diverges from.

Tactus emerges from Rudis populations in zones of moderate rock surface density, where electrophoretic drift in juveniles provides sufficient survival advantage to fix across generations. The divergence is documented in [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus]].

Rudis itself persists unchanged across the Erebian Era — its passive architecture, while inefficient relative to derived lineages, remains competitive wherever rock surface density is high enough that chance-settlement succeeds at viable rates.
