### Overview
Ferrovita Novus Liber is the derived lineage of [[Ferrovita Novus]], emerging at approximately 150 Million AC from [[Ferrovita Novus Parcus]] populations through progressive selection favouring longer methanogenic transit periods in the Fe³⁺-scarce cooler margins of Pocket A. Where Parcus uses methanogenesis only to bridge gaps between rock surfaces, Liber has crossed the threshold at which methanogenesis alone sustains continuous independent existence. It is the first life on Xenos genuinely untethered from a substrate — spending the majority of its existence as a free-floating organism within Pocket A fluid.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Open fluid zones of the cooler vent margins within [[Hydrology#Pocket A|Pocket A]], between and beyond rock surfaces. Periodically contacts Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces via electrophoretic drift for opportunistic iron reduction
- **Temperature:** ~110–130°C, consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus]] range
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus]] range
- **Chemistry:** Consistent with [[Ferrovita Novus#Environmental Niche]]. Liber operates across the full fluid volume of Pocket A's cooler margins rather than being constrained to rock surface proximity
- **Energy source:** Methanogenesis primary and continuous, iron reduction opportunistic upon rock contact

### Genetics
Follows [[Ferrovita#Genetics]] entirely.

Liber's metabolic shift relative to Parcus is expressed through more elaborate and efficient defect configurations supporting CO₂ reduction routing — a refinement of the same routing difference distinguishing all Novus from Firmus, not an architectural change to the genetic system.

### Membrane
Follows [[Ferrovita#Membrane]] entirely.

### Metabolism
Follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Metabolism]] entirely.

Methanogenesis runs continuously in Liber as the default metabolic state. Iron reduction activates opportunistically when electrophoretic drift brings the organism into contact with Fe³⁺-rich rock — the higher-yield pathway supplementing methanogenesis whenever the substrate is available, but survival no longer dependent on it.

Pathway switching follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Pathway Switching]] — the same passive concentration-driven routing, but with the metabolic centre of gravity inverted relative to Parcus. Where Parcus defaults to iron reduction and switches to methanogenesis under Fe³⁺ scarcity, Liber defaults to methanogenesis and switches to iron reduction under Fe³⁺ abundance.

### Electrophoresis and Navigation
Follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Electrophoresis and Navigation]] entirely.

Liber operates the electrophoretic mechanism continuously as its primary spatial orientation system — consistent with the later Novus pattern described in [[Ferrovita Novus#Electrophoresis and Navigation]]. A persistent low-level electrochemical charge allows continuous passive drift along Fe³⁺ gradients, periodically bringing Liber into contact with rock surfaces for iron reduction supplementation before detaching and resuming free drift.

This continuous electrophoretic state is sustainable in Liber in a way it is not in Parcus — because methanogenesis sustains metabolic activity independently of Fe³⁺, the electron accumulation driving electrophoretic charge is maintained continuously rather than only during transit depletion events.

### Reproduction
Follows [[Ferrovita#Reproduction]] entirely.

Liber juveniles inherit the methanogenesis fallback of all Novus lineages but with significantly greater methanogenic capacity than Parcus juveniles. Rock attachment remains the preferred establishment outcome — rock-bound Fe³⁺ provides higher energy yield — but Liber juveniles can sustain themselves on methanogenesis indefinitely if attachment is delayed, effectively eliminating the juvenile mortality window that constrains all Firmus lineages and early Novus.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Ferrovita Novus#Dormancy and Death]] entirely.

Dormancy in Liber requires simultaneous depletion of both H₂ and CO₂ — methanogenesis sustaining activity through Fe³⁺ scarcity events that would force Parcus toward dormancy. This makes Liber the most dormancy-resistant organism of the Erebian Era, with true dormancy representing an exceptionally rare event under normal Pocket A conditions.

### Ecological Character
Liber does not directly compete with [[Ferrovita Novus Parcus]] for the same resources — their differing metabolic centres of gravity place them in distinct ecological roles. Parcus remains the dominant rock-attached Novus lineage, exploiting Fe³⁺-rich surfaces with iron reduction as primary metabolism. Liber dominates the open fluid zones between rock surfaces, sustaining itself on methanogenesis and periodically supplementing with iron reduction on contact. The two lineages partition Pocket A's cooler margins spatially — Parcus at rock surfaces, Liber in the fluid between them.

By the end of the Erebian Era Liber represents the widest-ranging ecological presence of any organism on Xenos. Its chemical signature — methane output and diffuse Fe²⁺ from opportunistic iron reduction — is distributed across the full drift range of its electrophoretic movement, spreading biological influence into regions of Pocket A fluid that no sessile organism reaches. This distributed chemical presence will become significant during the Photian Era when capture disrupts the stable vent chemistry Pocket A has maintained across the entire rogue phase.

### Evolution
Ferrovita Novus Liber emerges at approximately 150 Million AC from [[Ferrovita Novus Parcus]] populations in the most Fe³⁺-scarce peripheral zones of Pocket A's cooler margins. The transition is not a single event but a gradual population-level shift — there is no sharp boundary between a late Parcus organism and an early Liber organism. The lineage distinction reflects the point at which methanogenesis becomes sufficient for continuous independent existence rather than merely extended transit survival.

Liber has no further divergence events within the Erebian Era. It enters the Photian Era as the most metabolically and spatially liberated organism Xenos has yet produced — a demonstration that Ferrovita's iron-sulphur lattice architecture, despite its geological character and glacial timescales, is capable of genuine evolutionary innovation given sufficient time and selective pressure.
