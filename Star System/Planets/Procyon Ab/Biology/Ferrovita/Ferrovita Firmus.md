### Overview
Ferrovita Firmus is the ancestral domain of [[Ferrovita]], representing the oldest and most chemically conservative form of life on Xenos. Where [[Ferrovita Novus]] diverged toward methanogenesis in cooler peripheral zones, Firmus remained committed to direct rock-bound iron reduction — the original metabolic strategy of all Ferrovita life. Firmus organisms are permanently attached to Fe³⁺-rich vent rock, growing across and consuming it over geological timescales. They are the closest living approximation to the first Ferrovita that ever existed.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Deepest, hottest hydrothermal vent zones within [[Hydrology#Pocket A|Pocket A]]
- **Temperature:** ~200–250°C, locally higher at direct vent interfaces
- **Pressure:** Extreme — deepest subsurface zones
- **Chemistry:** Highest Fe³⁺ concentration relative to rest of Pocket A, strongest H₂ flux from vent outflow
- **Energy source:** Direct Fe³⁺ reduction from rock substrate, supplemented by dissolved Fe³⁺ from fluid

Firmus occupies the zones of highest chemical energy on Xenos — direct vent interfaces where Fe³⁺-rich rock is continuously refreshed by hydrothermal fluid circulation and crustal upwelling. This niche is the most energetically rich but also the most physically extreme, limiting Firmus to organisms with the most robust lattice structures.

### Genetics
Follows [[Ferrovita#Genetics]] entirely. Firmus represents the unmodified founding lattice architecture — its defect pattern distribution is the reference against which Novus's methanogenic routing differences are measured.

### Membrane
Follows [[Ferrovita#Membrane]] entirely.

The rock-facing surface of the pyrite shell integrates chemically with the rock substrate, creating a continuous mineral interface where Fe³⁺ in the rock's crystal structure is directly accessible to Ferrovita's electron transport network. As Fe³⁺ is reduced to Fe²⁺ at this interface the rock's mineral structure destabilises locally — iron ions are liberated, either incorporated into the growing Firmus lattice or expelled as dissolved Fe²⁺ into Pocket A fluid. The rock face is consumed progressively as Firmus grows across it.

Growth proceeds across the rock face in the direction of highest remaining Fe³⁺ concentration, guided by the electrochemical gradient between the organism's reduction sites and unprocessed rock. Regions already depleted of Fe³⁺ see growth slow or cease entirely while adjacent Fe³⁺-rich regions continue to be actively consumed.

### Metabolism
Follows [[Ferrovita#Metabolism]] entirely.

**Fe³⁺ source** — primarily rock-bound rather than dissolved. The attachment interface is the primary reduction zone, with the electron transport network directing the majority of electron flux toward rock-facing lattice regions. Dissolved Fe³⁺ from Pocket A fluid contributes secondarily, accessed through the fluid-facing shell surface.

**H₂ supply** — Firmus occupies zones of highest vent H₂ flux, giving it the most abundant hydrogen supply of any Ferrovita. H₂ diffuses passively through microfractures across the entire shell surface.

**Energy output** — Firmus generates the highest sustained metabolic output of any Ferrovita domain, reflecting its privileged access to rock-bound Fe³⁺. This drives faster lattice accrual and more frequent bud production relative to [[Ferrovita Novus]], though both remain slow on absolute timescales.

### Electrophoresis and Navigation
Follows [[Ferrovita#Electrophoresis and Navigation]] entirely. Application across Firmus lineages is documented in [[Ferrovita Firmus Rudis]], [[Ferrovita Firmus Tactus]], and [[Ferrovita Firmus Mobilis]].

### Reproduction
Follows [[Ferrovita#Reproduction]] entirely.

Firmus juveniles are entirely dependent on dissolved Fe³⁺ from Pocket A fluid during the transitional phase — a scarce resource relative to the rock-bound Fe³⁺ the parent accesses. Juvenile mortality is high. Electrophoretic drift toward Fe³⁺-rich rock surfaces is the primary survival mechanism from Tactus onward. See [[Ferrovita#Juvenile Phase]] and [[Ferrovita#Electrophoresis and Navigation]].

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Ferrovita#Dormancy and Death]] entirely.

Firmus-specific dormancy trigger is vent inactivity — reduction in H₂ flux and Fe³⁺ availability at the rock interface causes progressive gradient decay. Given Firmus occupies the most geologically active zones of Pocket A, vent inactivity is relatively rare but not absent over billion-year timescales.

The oldest Firmus individuals may have persisted since the earliest Erebian Era, surviving multiple vent cycles through repeated dormancy and recovery. These ancient organisms carry the most accumulated lattice defect variation of any life on Xenos — their electrochemical architecture is a geological record of Xenos's entire biological history compressed into mineral structure.

### Ecological Character
Consumptive, stationary, and chemically dominant.

Firmus is the primary geological consumer of Xenos — the organism most directly responsible for the progressive chemical transformation of Pocket A's vent core environments. Permanently attached to Fe³⁺-rich vent rock and consuming it continuously across geological timescales, Firmus does not merely exploit the chemical energy of its environment but physically dismantles it, liberating iron from mineral structure and releasing it as dissolved Fe²⁺ into surrounding fluid.

Individual Firmus organisms may persist for millions of years without dormancy in stable vent zones, continuously reducing rock-bound Fe³⁺ across an ever-expanding attachment interface. Ancient Firmus colonies represent the most sustained and concentrated sites of biological rock consumption on Xenos — their attachment interfaces are zones of progressive mineral destabilisation extending outward from the original settlement point across geological time.

Fe²⁺ output from Firmus metabolism is the single largest biological contribution to Pocket A's fluid chemistry. The extremely high Fe³⁺ concentrations available at direct vent interfaces, combined with Firmus's privileged access to rock-bound rather than dissolved iron, means Firmus populations generate Fe²⁺ at rates significantly exceeding those of Novus populations in the cooler margins. This output saturates the local fluid environment and propagates outward through Pocket A, contributing to the basin-wide Fe²⁺ enrichment that defines the fluid's reducing character.

Within the vent core zones Firmus dominates without meaningful competition — no other organism on Xenos during the Erebian Era can survive the temperatures and pressures Firmus inhabits, and no other organism can access rock-bound Fe³⁺ with comparable efficiency. Firmus's ecological dominance in its niche is total and unchallenged across the entire Erebian Era.

### Evolution
Ferrovita Firmus is the founding domain of the Ferrovita radix — the original iron-reduction-only architecture from which all subsequent Ferrovita divergence is measured. It emerges in the earliest Erebian Era in the deepest, most Fe³⁺-rich vent zones of Pocket A, where conditions select for the most robust lattice structures and the most direct rock-interface metabolic strategy.

Firmus diversifies internally across three lineages — each a single innovation building on the last, progressing from chance-dependent juvenile settlement through directed navigation to adult mobility. Each lineage documents its own divergence in detail.

At the Mobilis grade the domain diverges — marginal Mobilis populations in the cooler vent periphery develop methanogenic capacity, producing [[Ferrovita Novus]]. Firmus itself continues unchanged beyond this point, its iron-reduction-only strategy remaining the dominant metabolic approach in the vent core zones it has always occupied.
