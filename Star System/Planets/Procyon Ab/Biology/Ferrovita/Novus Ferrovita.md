- **Habitat:** Cooler vent margins (~110–130°C) within the same vents as [[Firmus Ferrovita]]
- **Metabolism:**
    - **Primary:** Hydrogen oxidation (H₂ as electron donor)
    - **Secondary (reduced):** Iron reduction
- **Structure:** Similar lattice to Firmus, with no major morphological divergence
- **Energy source:** Mixed gradients (hydrogen + iron/sulfur chemistry)

Novus [[Ferrovita]] arose not through migration, but through **local variation within the same environment**. Random evolution in their defect-encoded lattice altered electron transport behaviour, enabling the use of hydrogen as a energy source.

In these slightly cooler zones, where iron gradients are weaker but hydrogen persists in trapped pockets, this caused an evolutionary advantage for Novus [[Ferrovita]] to live in these regions compared to their [[Firmus Ferrovita]]. Over time, Hydrogen oxidation became the dominant metabloc process while iron reduction was retained in a diminished role
