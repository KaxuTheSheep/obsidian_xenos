### Overview
Ferrovita are extremophilic, mineral-based lifeforms inhabiting the deepest hydrothermal vent zones of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]]. Existing at the boundary between biology and [[Geology]], they are not built from organic polymers but from structured iron–sulfur minerals. Their bodies grow directly from the surrounding rock, forming slow-living, crystalline systems that extract energy from redox gradients in iron-rich fluids. To outside observers, Ferrovita are nearly indistinguishable from vent formations — living structures embedded within the planet’s crust.

### Genetics
Ferrovita encodes information not in molecular sequences but in the **three dimensional pattern of electrochemical properties across its iron-sulphur lattice**. This system has no analogue in carbon-based life — genetics, metabolism, and structure are not separate systems but a single unified electrochemical phenomenon.

#### Information Storage
A perfect FeS₂ crystal lattice has every atom in precise geometric arrangement, conducting electrons uniformly. Ferrovita's lattice is never perfect — it is defined by controlled imperfection. Four categories of defect carry information:

**Vacancy defects** — missing atoms creating gaps in the conductive network. These interrupt electron flow pathways, functioning analogously to open switches in a circuit.

**Substitution defects** — trace metal ions from Pocket A fluid, primarily nickel, copper, and zinc, substituting for iron at specific lattice positions. Each substitution alters the electrochemical properties of that node, changing how electrons move through the surrounding region.

**Oxidation state variations** — Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺ distributed at specific positions throughout the lattice rather than uniformly. Since Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺ have different electron configurations they create localised regions of differing conductivity, functioning as variable resistances within the circuit.

**Geometric distortions** — atoms slightly displaced from ideal positions by mechanical stress or thermal fluctuation, subtly altering bond angles and therefore electron orbital overlap across affected regions.

The collective three dimensional map of these defects determines the precise pattern of electron flow pathways through the entire lattice — which regions conduct strongly, which resist, which redirect. This pattern is Ferrovita's genome. Different patterns produce different metabolic behaviours, different growth tendencies, and different catalytic properties at specific lattice regions. The organism is simultaneously a battery, a circuit board, and a self-replicating mineral — metabolism and genetics are not merely linked but are the same physical phenomenon expressed differently.

#### Defect Propagation — Inheritance
New lattice material accretes throughout the entire structure simultaneously. Iron is supplied as Fe²⁺ from rock reduction at the rock-facing interface, moving inward. Sulphur is supplied from H₂S diffusing passively through microfractures from Pocket A fluid, moving inward from the fluid-facing side. These two streams meet and combine across the whole lattice, crystallising new FeS₂ wherever local conditions permit.

Existing defects act as crystallisation templates — the irregular geometry of the existing lattice guides how new material crystallises around it. Vacancies in the existing structure create regions of altered surface energy that bias new crystal growth toward similar vacancy formation nearby. Substitution defects alter local chemistry in ways that favour incorporation of the same trace metals into adjacent new material. Oxidation state patterns propagate because existing Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ distributions influence the redox state of newly incorporated iron.

New lattice growth therefore inherits the existing defect pattern with reasonable fidelity — not through any active copying mechanism but through the passive physical and chemical influence of existing structure on new crystallisation. The circuit replicates itself by being the template its own growth follows.

#### Variation Sources
Inheritance fidelity is high but not absolute. Four mechanisms introduce variation:

**Uneven iron supply** — iron reduction at the rock interface is spatially variable, producing regions of faster and slower Fe²⁺ supply. Areas of iron scarcity during growth produce vacancy defects at higher rates than the parent pattern specifies, altering local electron flow.

**Uneven sulphur supply** — H₂S diffusion through microfractures is not uniform across the shell surface. Regions of lower sulphur availability during crystallisation produce sulphur vacancy defects or incorporate alternative sulphur compounds, distorting the inherited pattern locally.

**Temperature fluctuations** — Pocket A's 150–250°C temperature range causes thermal expansion and contraction of the lattice. Fluctuations during active growth alter crystallisation geometry, introducing geometric distortions not present in the parent structure.

**Trace metal availability** — substitution defects depend on which trace metals are present in Pocket A fluid at the moment of crystallisation. Fluctuations in nickel, copper, or zinc concentration produce different substitution patterns than the parent lattice, altering electron flow properties at affected nodes.

#### Replication Speed
Lattice growth is inherently slow — constrained by iron reduction rate at the rock interface, H₂S diffusion rate through the shell, and the thermodynamics of crystal growth at 150–250°C. Full organism replication through budding operates on timescales of **years to centuries** depending on local chemical availability and organism scale. This is orders of magnitude slower than [[Sulphovita]]'s days to weeks replication cycle, reflecting Ferrovita's fundamental character — stable, ancient, and slow relative to all life that will eventually descend from [[Sulphovita]]'s lineage.

### Membrane
- **Outer boundary:** Porous pyrite (FeS₂) composite shell
- **Form:** Irregular crystalline masses, veins, or nodular growths
- **Scale:** Microscopic seeds to metre-scale colonies
- **Appearance:** Metallic gold to dark bronze, visually continuous with surrounding rock
The pyrite shell is an active mineral interface rather than a passive barrier. The same electron transport network that drives Ferrovita's metabolism extends into and regulates the shell itself — creating electrochemical gradients that actively pull Fe³⁺ inward while expelling waste products outward. Neutral molecules — primarily H₂ and H₂S from Pocket A fluid — diffuse inward passively through microfractures and lattice discontinuities, supplementing the actively transported materials.

The shell grows simultaneously at two interfaces. At the fluid-facing surface new pyrite accretes from dissolved iron in Pocket A fluid, expanding outward. At the rock-facing surface the shell integrates directly with Fe³⁺-rich vent rock, growing inward as iron is consumed and the underlying mineral structure destabilises. Growth is uneven across both surfaces — regions of higher chemical gradient accrete faster, producing an irregular, asymmetric shell morphology.

This uneven growth is the foundation of Ferrovita's reproductive mechanism. Regions of accelerated accrual form outward protrusions — buds — that develop their own internal lattice structure and redox gradients inherited from the parent. At sufficient mass a bud generates mechanical stress at its connection point with the parent shell. When this stress exceeds the structural integrity of the connecting material the bud separates, settling nearby and attaching to available rock surface to begin independent growth. See [[Firmus Ferrovita]] and [[Novus Ferrovita]] for sub-lineage specific variations on this process.

Structurally the organism remains inseparable from its substrate — the rock-facing surface and the rock itself are chemically continuous at the attachment interface. Ferrovita does not sit on rock. At its base it becomes rock, consuming and replacing mineral structure as it grows.








### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Deep hydrothermal vent systems
- **Temperature:** ~150–250°C (locally higher in microenvironments)
- **Pressure:** Extreme (crustal/oceanic depths)
- **Chemistry:** Iron-rich, sulfuric, highly acidic fluids
- **Energy source:** Iron redox gradients driven by vent chemistry

Ferrovita occupy the most chemically aggressive zones, where fluid interfaces create persistent energy gradients. Their existence is tightly coupled to these environments — they do not move, but grow where conditions permit.

### Metabolism
- **Primary process:** Iron reduction–oxidation cycling
- **Electron acceptors/donors:** Fe³⁺ / Fe²⁺ and sulphur compounds
- **Mechanism:** Electron transport across the mineral lattice
- **Energy use:** Maintaining structural growth and internal redox gradients
Rather than internal biochemical pathways, Ferrovita metabolism occurs across their entire structure. The lattice itself acts as a conductive network, channelling electrons between regions of differing chemical potential. Energy is not stored in molecules but in maintained gradients and structural ordering.

### Reproduction and Growth
- **Mode:** Accretive budding and lattice fragmentation
- **Timescale:** Extremely slow — years to centuries per replication cycle
- **Process:**
    - Local lattice expansion
    - Formation of semi-independent growth nodes
    - Eventual structural separation under stress or environmental shift
Reproduction is indistinguishable from growth until separation occurs. “Offspring” are simply regions of the lattice that achieve sufficient independence to persist under slightly different conditions.

### System Role
Within the ecosystems of [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]], Ferrovita act as:
- **Primary redox mediators** in deep vent chemistry
- **Geochemical stabilizers**, buffering iron and sulfur cycles
- **Substrate creators** for more complex (possibly organic) life
They may represent a parallel biosphere — one that never transitioned to carbon-based chemistry, remaining instead in a state close to Abiogenesis.

### The Living Stone
Ferrovita challenge the distinction between life and non-life. They do not move, breathe, or think in any recognizable way. They grow, persist, and slowly evolve — their “thought” expressed only as changes in structure over geological time.
To most observers, they are indistinguishable from rock.  
To the planet, they may be as alive as anything that has ever existed.
