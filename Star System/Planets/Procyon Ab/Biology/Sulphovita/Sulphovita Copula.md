### Overview
Sulphovita Copula is the first major divergent domain of [[Sulphovita]], emerging from peripheral Pocket B populations experiencing chronic H₂S scarcity relative to available methane. Rather than remaining dependent on H₂S oxidation to prime methane activation, Copula evolved a dedicated iron-coordination mechanism capable of cycling Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ internally to destabilise the methane C-H bond directly. This makes Copula the first Sulphovita domain to develop a meaningful iron dependency, decoupling methane activation from H₂S availability for the first time in Xenian biological history.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Peripheral zones of Pocket B, furthest from active vent outflow
- **Temperature:** ~50–80°C, cooler than baseline Sulphovita zones
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita]] range
- **Chemistry:** Reduced H₂S availability relative to vent core zones, higher relative Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability, methane remains abundant
- **Energy source:** Methane oxidation via iron-coordinated activation. H₂S oxidation retained opportunistically or abandoned entirely depending on lineage — documented in [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] and [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]]

### Metabolism
Follows [[Sulphovita#Metabolism]] entirely, with the exception that the H₂S priming dependency for methane activation is replaced or supplemented by iron-coordinated activation via Ferric coordinase. The overall methane oxidation reaction remains unchanged:

**CH₄ + SO₄²⁻ → H₂S + CO₂ + H₂O**

Only the activation step differs from baseline Sulphovita.

#### Iron-Coordinated Methane Activation
Baseline [[Sulphovita]] activates methane by redirecting energy from concurrent H₂S oxidation to overcome the C-H bond activation barrier via Methanyl activase. This creates a hard dependency — without sufficient H₂S, methane oxidation cannot initiate regardless of methane availability.

Copula resolves this through a dedicated peptide module — **Ferric coordinase** — which maintains a small internal Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ redox cycle. The transition between oxidation states generates localised radical chemistry sufficient to destabilise the methane C-H bond, replacing H₂S priming entirely as the activation mechanism. Ferric coordinase operates independently of H₂S availability — methane oxidation can initiate and sustain itself as long as internal iron concentrations remain sufficient.

##### Iron Retention
At Pocket B's Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ concentrations of ~15–25 mmol/L and ~10–20 mmol/L respectively, Copula cannot afford to lose iron to the surrounding fluid. A dedicated **iron retention module** embedded in the sulphonolipid membrane actively imports Fe²⁺ from Pocket B fluid and prevents its expulsion, maintaining internal iron concentrations sufficient to sustain Ferric coordinase activity.

This active retention works against a concentration gradient — internal iron concentrations must remain significantly above ambient to sustain the activation cycle. This represents a continuous metabolic overhead absent in baseline Sulphovita, making Copula less efficient in H₂S-rich environments where baseline Sulphovita runs methane activation cheaply via H₂S priming.

#### Pathway Behaviour
The relationship between H₂S oxidation and methane oxidation differs across Copula lineages. At the domain level, Copula is defined by the capacity to activate methane independently of H₂S — whether H₂S oxidation is retained as a supplementary pathway or abandoned entirely is a lineage-level distinction documented in [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] and [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]].

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

#### Copula-Specific Dormancy
Copula occupies the peripheral zones of Pocket B furthest from active vent outflow — the most chemically variable and substrate-scarce regions of the Sulphovita biosphere. Dormancy is significantly more common in Copula populations than in [[Sulphovita Primalis]], reflecting the greater chemical instability of peripheral zones.

Dormancy triggering and resilience differs significantly between lineages based on whether H₂S oxidation has been retained — documented in [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] and [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]].

#### Copula-Specific Death
The primary death risk for Copula lineages is iron scarcity rather than vent extinction. Pocket B's Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ concentrations represent a narrower margin than the sulphur and methane availability Primalis depends on. Localised iron depletion through Copula's own iron retention activity — multiple organisms competing for the same iron pool in a peripheral zone with limited vent replenishment — represents a genuine population-level death risk unique to Copula lineages.

### Ecological Character
Sulphovita Copula occupies the peripheral chemical gradient of Pocket B, filling zones that baseline Sulphovita cannot sustain itself in. Its iron-coordination capability allows methane oxidation to continue where H₂S availability would strand Primalis entirely, expanding the viable Sulphovita biosphere outward from vent-proximal zones toward the furthest margins of Pocket B.

Spatial partitioning between Copula and baseline Sulphovita is maintained by differing chemical tolerances rather than physical barriers — baseline Sulphovita dominates vent-proximal H₂S-rich zones, Copula lineages occupy intermediate and peripheral zones across a chemical gradient with competition at every boundary. The overlap zones between Primalis and Copula are chemically dynamic, with local H₂S and Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ fluctuations continuously shifting competitive advantage between lineages.

### Evolution
Sulphovita Copula emerges at approximately 500 Million AC from peripheral [[Sulphovita Primalis]] populations experiencing chronic H₂S scarcity. The divergence was gradual — Ferric coordinase emerging through accumulated peptide variation in populations already operating at the margins of H₂S availability, where even modest iron-coordination capacity provided meaningful survival advantage. There was no single divergence event but a progressive shift across populations already stressed by their chemical environment.

Copula diverges into two lineages across the Erebian Era, each representing a different evolutionary commitment to iron-coordinated activation. Each lineage documents its own divergence in detail.

By the end of the Erebian Era one Copula lineage has gone extinct — driven out by its own descendant — leaving Solutus as the sole surviving Copula lineage entering the Photian Era in the most precarious ecological position of any Sulphovita organism.
