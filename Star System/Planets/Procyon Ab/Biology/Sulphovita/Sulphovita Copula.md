### Overview
[[Sulphovita]] Copula is the first major divergent lineage of [[Sulphovita]], emerging from peripheral Pocket B populations experiencing chronic H₂S scarcity relative to available methane. Rather than remaining dependent on H₂S oxidation to prime methane activation, Copula lineages evolved a dedicated iron-coordination mechanism — a peptide module capable of cycling Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ internally to destabilise the methane C-H bond directly. This makes Copula the first [[Sulphovita]] lineage to develop a meaningful iron dependency, and [[Sulphovita]] Solutus the first organism on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] to completely abandon a founding metabolic pathway.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Peripheral zones of Pocket B, furthest from active vent outflow
- **Temperature:** ~50–80°C, cooler than baseline [[Sulphovita]] zones
- **Chemistry:** Reduced H₂S availability relative to vent core zones, higher relative Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability, methane remains abundant
- **Energy source:** Methane oxidation via iron-coordinated activation, H₂S oxidation retained opportunistically (Incitor) or abandoned entirely (Solutus)

### Metabolic Evolution
Baseline [[Sulphovita Primalis]] activates methane by borrowing energy from concurrent H₂S oxidation to overcome the C-H bond activation barrier. This coupling creates a hard dependency — without sufficient H₂S, methane oxidation cannot initiate regardless of methane availability.

[[Sulphovita]] Copula resolves this through a fundamentally different activation mechanism. A dedicated peptide module — **Ferric coordinase** — maintains a small internal Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ redox cycle. The transition between oxidation states generates localised radical chemistry sufficient to destabilise the methane C-H bond, replacing H₂S priming entirely as the activation mechanism.

The overall methane oxidation reaction remains unchanged:

**CH₄ + SO₄²⁻ → H₂S + CO₂ + H₂O**

Only the activation step differs — instead of H₂S oxidation energy priming Methanyl activase, Ferric coordinase provides activation energy through iron redox cycling.

#### Iron Retention
At Pocket B's Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ concentrations of ~15–25 mmol/L and ~10–20 mmol/L respectively, Copula cannot afford to lose iron to the surrounding fluid. A dedicated **iron retention module** embedded in the sulphonolipid membrane actively imports Fe²⁺ from Pocket B fluid and prevents its expulsion, maintaining internal iron concentrations sufficient to sustain Ferric coordinase activity.

This active retention works against a concentration gradient — internal iron concentrations must remain significantly above ambient to sustain the activation cycle. This represents a continuous metabolic overhead absent in baseline [[Sulphovita]], making Copula less efficient in H₂S-rich environments where baseline [[Sulphovita]] runs methane activation cheaply via H₂S priming.

### [[Sulphovita]] Incitor
*Emerges ~500 Million AC*
#### Overview
Incitor is the opportunist stage of the Copula lineage — the first [[Sulphovita]] to possess iron-coordinated methane activation, but not yet fully committed to it. Ferric coordinase is present and functional but represents a relatively recent evolutionary innovation, not yet refined to the point where it can fully displace H₂S priming under all conditions.

#### Metabolic Strategy
Incitor switches contextually between activation mechanisms based on local chemical availability:

- **High Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, low H₂S** — Ferric coordinase operates as primary activation mechanism, iron-coordinated methane oxidation dominant
- **High H₂S, low Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺** — H₂S priming operates as primary activation mechanism, baseline [[Sulphovita]] behaviour
- **High Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, high H₂S** — both mechanisms available, organism exploits whichever local conditions favour, maximising methane oxidation rate
- **Low Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, low H₂S** — metabolic activity reduced, organism enters low activity state

This opportunism is Incitor's defining ecological character — it is not a specialist but an organism in transition, capable of exploiting chemical conditions that would strand baseline [[Sulphovita]] while retaining full baseline capability where H₂S is available.

#### Competitive Position
Incitor outcompetes baseline [[Sulphovita]] in iron-rich, H₂S-poor peripheral zones. In H₂S-rich zones baseline [[Sulphovita]]'s lower metabolic overhead gives it a competitive advantage — Incitor pays the cost of iron retention regardless of whether Ferric coordinase is actively running, a fixed overhead that baseline [[Sulphovita]] never incurs.

### [[Sulphovita]] Solutus
*Emerges ~320 Million AC*

#### Overview
Solutus is the specialist stage of the Copula lineage and the direct descendant of Incitor populations in the most H₂S-depleted peripheral zones. Where Incitor retained H₂S oxidation as a fallback, Solutus has abandoned it entirely — the first organism on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] to completely relinquish a founding metabolic pathway.

#### Abandonment Mechanism
Solutus does not lose H₂S oxidation through any active deletion mechanism — [[Sulphovita]]'s peptide architecture has no genome from which modules can be removed. Instead, abandonment occurs through **distributional marginalisation**. As Ferric coordinase becomes increasingly refined across Incitor generations, iron-coordinated activation progressively dominates the internal peptide distribution. H₂S oxidation modules, no longer contributing meaningfully to energy acquisition, accumulate replication errors that go unselected against. Their representation in the peptide network falls across generations until they drop below the functional threshold required to sustain the pathway. The modules are not erased — they are crowded out, becoming vestigial remnants of insufficient concentration to catalyse meaningful chemistry.

#### Metabolic Strategy
Solutus operates exclusively on iron-coordinated methane oxidation. H₂S encountered in the environment is not metabolised — it passes through membrane transport systems without triggering oxidation pathways. Sulphate remains the terminal electron acceptor and the overall reaction is unchanged, but activation is entirely dependent on Ferric coordinase.

This makes Solutus the most metabolically specialised organism of the Erebian Era — and the most vulnerable to iron scarcity. A drop in Pocket B Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability below the threshold required to sustain Ferric coordinase cycling causes immediate and unrecoverable metabolic failure, with no fallback pathway available.

#### Competitive Position
Solutus dominates the most extreme peripheral zones of Pocket B where H₂S availability is lowest and iron availability is highest. It is outcompeted by Incitor in intermediate zones where H₂S fluctuates sufficiently to make pathway flexibility valuable, and by baseline [[Sulphovita]] in vent-proximal zones where H₂S is abundant. Solutus occupies the narrowest ecological niche of any [[Sulphovita]] lineage — but within that niche it is unrivalled.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

#### Copula-Specific Dormancy
Copula occupies the peripheral zones of Pocket B furthest from active vent outflow — the most chemically variable and substrate-scarce regions of the [[Sulphovita]] biosphere. Dormancy is significantly more common in Copula populations than in [[Sulphovita Primalis]], reflecting the greater chemical instability of peripheral zones.

**[[Sulphovita]] Copula Incitor** — while it persists — has the most resilient dormancy profile of any Copula lineage. Its dual-mechanism architecture means that if Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability drops below Ferric coordinase threshold, Incitor can partially sustain metabolic activity on H₂S priming while iron concentrations recover. Conversely if H₂S drops below priming threshold, iron-coordinated activation can sustain reduced metabolic activity independently. True full dormancy in Incitor requires both mechanisms to fail simultaneously — a less common scenario than single-substrate depletion. This metabolic flexibility extends Incitor's effective dormancy resistance beyond what either mechanism alone would provide.

**[[Sulphovita]] Copula Solutus** has the most precarious dormancy profile of any organism in the Erebian Era biosphere. With H₂S oxidation completely absent from its peptide network, Solutus has no fallback when Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability drops below Ferric coordinase threshold. Dormancy is triggered immediately and exclusively by iron scarcity — there is no partial metabolic state, no alternative pathway to slow the decline. Once dormant Solutus degrades at the same rate as any [[Sulphovita]], but enters dormancy more readily and with no recovery flexibility if iron availability does not return within the dormancy window.

#### Copula-Specific Death
The primary death risk for Copula lineages is iron scarcity rather than vent extinction. Pocket B's Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ concentrations of ~15–25 mmol/L and ~10–20 mmol/L respectively are sufficient under normal conditions but represent a narrower margin than the sulphur and methane availability that Primalis depends on. Localised iron depletion through Copula's own iron retention activity — multiple organisms competing for the same iron pool in a peripheral zone with limited vent replenishment — represents a genuine population-level death risk unique to Copula lineages.

For Solutus specifically, iron depletion below Ferric coordinase threshold produces a death cascade with no recovery pathway. Unlike Primalis, which retains dual-pathway flexibility even in dormancy onset, Solutus transitions from full metabolic activity to irreversible collapse without intermediate states. This makes Solutus population dynamics characteristically volatile — stable under favourable iron conditions, catastrophically vulnerable to any sustained iron depletion event. This vulnerability is the defining ecological limitation of the most specialised organism of the Erebian Era, and will become critically significant during the Photian Era when environmental upheaval disrupts the stable chemical gradients Solutus depends on entirely.
### Relationship to Baseline [[Sulphovita]]
[[Sulphovita]] Copula descended from peripheral baseline populations of [[Sulphovita Primalis]] experiencing chronic H₂S scarcity. The divergence was gradual — Ferric coordinase emerging through accumulated peptide variation in populations already operating at the margins of H₂S availability, where even modest iron-coordination capacity provided meaningful survival advantage. There was no single divergence event but a progressive shift across populations already stressed by their chemical environment.

Spatial partitioning between baseline [[Sulphovita]] and Copula lineages is maintained by their differing chemical tolerances — baseline [[Sulphovita]] dominates vent-proximal H₂S-rich zones, Incitor occupies intermediate zones, Solutus the most peripheral iron-rich regions. The three populations exist across a chemical gradient rather than in discrete separated territories, with overlap zones hosting competition between lineages at every boundary.
