### Overview
Sulphovita Primalis Purus is the undifferentiated founding lineage of [[Sulphovita Primalis]] and the original unmodified biochemical architecture of the [[Sulphovita]] radix. No metabolic modifications, no structural departures from the founding peptide network organisation. Purus is the reference point against which all subsequent Sulphovita divergence is measured.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Vent-proximal zones of Pocket B, within the broader [[Sulphovita Primalis]] range. Favours intermediate-rich fluid zones generated by high density Primalis populations
- **Temperature:** ~80–120°C
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] range
- **Chemistry:** Highest H₂S and methane availability within Pocket B. Elevated S⁰ and thiosulphate concentrations in zones of high population density
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Sulphovita Primalis#Environmental Niche]] entirely.

#### Loose Module Distribution
Purus's defining metabolic trait is its loosely distributed peptide network — sulphur oxidation modules are spatially unconstrained, freely interfacing with the surrounding fluid environment. Intermediates produced at each oxidation step are released into the organism's internal fluid before capture by the next module, creating opportunity for escape across the membrane before processing completes.

This produces the characteristic leaky sulphur cycling that defines Primalis inefficiency, but simultaneously allows Purus to scavenge external S⁰ and thiosulphate intermediates from the surrounding fluid efficiently. In intermediate-rich zones generated by high density Primalis populations, this loose module distribution becomes a competitive advantage over more internally-oriented lineages.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita Primalis#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

Purus enters dormancy with a loosely distributed peptide network. Module density is lower than Condensus at any given moment, meaning the network reaches the minimum functional threshold faster during degradation. Purus dormancy windows are therefore marginally shorter than Condensus under equivalent conditions.

### Ecological Character
Purus dominates intermediate-rich fluid zones within Pocket B's vent-proximal regions. Where high density Primalis populations have accumulated significant S⁰ and thiosulphate in the surrounding fluid, Purus's loose module distribution allows it to exploit these resources more efficiently than lineages with tighter internal clustering.

Population-level diversity is maintained entirely through the inherent asymmetry of [[Sulphovita]] reproduction, generating continuous biochemical variation that underpins Purus's adaptability to chemical fluctuation. Purus populations are the most biochemically heterogeneous within Primalis — no clustering innovation constrains daughter inheritance, so each reproductive cycle produces genuine variation across the population.

Purus is outcompeted in clean sulphate-limited zones by [[Sulphovita Primalis Condensus]], whose tighter module clustering reduces shared sulphate depletion. The two lineages are chemically entangled — Purus generates the intermediates Condensus cannot scavenge efficiently, while Condensus's sulphate preservation partially benefits Purus by slowing depletion of their shared sulphate pool.

### Evolution
Sulphovita Primalis Purus has no divergence event of its own — it is the founding lineage, the unmodified baseline. Its evolutionary history is defined by what emerges from it rather than what it diverges from.

Condensus emerges from Purus populations under high density sulphate-limited conditions, representing the first internal partitioning of the Primalis domain. The divergence is documented in [[Sulphovita Primalis Condensus]].

Purus itself persists unchanged across the Erebian Era — its loosely distributed architecture, while metabolically inefficient relative to derived lineages, remains competitive wherever intermediate scavenging outweighs sulphate conservation as the dominant selective pressure.
