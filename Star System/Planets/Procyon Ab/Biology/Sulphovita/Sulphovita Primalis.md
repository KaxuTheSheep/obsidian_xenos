### Overview

### Ecological Character
Adaptive, chemically integrated, and abundant.

[[Sulphovita]] Primalis occupies the most chemically rich zones of Pocket B ‚ÄĒ vent-proximal regions with highest substrate availability. Its defining domain-level trait is adaptability through biochemical diversity ‚ÄĒ each reproductive cycle generates genuine variation across the population, allowing rapid population-level response to chemical fluctuation.

Primalis dominates vent-proximal zones where H‚āāS is abundant. It is outcompeted in H‚āāS-poor peripheral zones by [[Sulphovita]] Copula lineages, which operate independently of H‚āāS priming. Within Primalis, the two lineages partition the chemical environment based on sulphate availability and intermediate concentration ‚ÄĒ Condensus dominating clean sulphate-limited zones, Purus persisting where external intermediates are abundant.

### [[Sulphovita]] Primalis Purus
The undifferentiated founding lineage of [[Sulphovita]] Primalis. Purus represents the original unmodified [[Sulphovita]] biochemical architecture ‚ÄĒ no metabolic modifications, no structural departures from the founding peptide network organisation. It is the reference point against which all subsequent [[Sulphovita]] divergence is measured.

Purus's defining trait is its loosely distributed peptide network ‚ÄĒ sulphur oxidation modules are spatially unconstrained, freely interfacing with the surrounding fluid environment. This produces the characteristic leaky sulphur cycling that defines Primalis inefficiency, but simultaneously allows Purus to scavenge external S‚Āį and thiosulphate intermediates from the surrounding fluid efficiently. In intermediate-rich zones generated by high density Primalis populations, Purus's loose module distribution becomes a competitive advantage over more internally-oriented lineages.

Population-level diversity is maintained entirely through the inherent asymmetry of [[Sulphovita]] reproduction, generating continuous biochemical variation that underpins Purus's adaptability to chemical fluctuation.

### [[Sulphovita]] Primalis Condensus
*Emerges ~550 Million AC*
Condensus emerges from Purus populations in high density sulphate-limited zones through progressive tightening of sulphur oxidation module clustering within the peptide network. Unlike domain-level divergences which involve novel biochemical mechanisms, Condensus represents a spatial reorganisation of existing Primalis machinery ‚ÄĒ the same Sulphide oxidase, Sulphur oxidase, and Sulphite oxidase modules present in Purus, repositioned into tighter functional proximity.

#### The Clustering Innovation
In Purus, sulphur oxidation modules are distributed loosely across the peptide network. Intermediates ‚ÄĒ S‚Āį and SO‚āÉ¬≤‚ĀĽ ‚ÄĒ produced at each oxidation step are released into the organism's internal fluid before being captured by the next module, creating opportunity for escape across the membrane before processing completes. In Condensus, progressive clustering brings successive oxidation modules into sufficiently close proximity that intermediates are handed off directly between modules before they can diffuse away. The result is a near-complete internal sulphur cycle ‚ÄĒ intermediates are processed to sulphate before escaping, significantly reducing outward sulphur flux.

#### Consequences of Clustering
**Sulphate preservation** ‚ÄĒ reduced intermediate escape means less S‚Āį and thiosulphate accumulating in surrounding fluid. In high density zones where multiple organisms compete for the same sulphate pool, Condensus degrades the shared chemical environment significantly less than Purus, compounding its competitive advantage over generations.

**Proto-division of labour** ‚ÄĒ the sulphur processing cluster becomes a distinct functional zone within the peptide network, operating semi-independently from surrounding metabolic machinery. This is the first instance of regionalised biochemical identity within a single [[Sulphovita]] organism ‚ÄĒ not yet a true division of labour but a structural precursor to it.

**Consistent daughter inheritance** ‚ÄĒ clustered modules replicate in close proximity, making it more likely that daughter organisms inherit the full functional cluster intact rather than a scrambled subset. Condensus daughters are more biochemically consistent than Purus daughters, partially counteracting the inherent asymmetry of [[Sulphovita]] reproduction. This reduces population-level variation but increases reliable transmission of the clustering innovation across generations.

#### Limitations
Condensus's inward-oriented module clustering makes it less efficient at processing external sulphur intermediates from the surrounding fluid. While Condensus retains the biochemical capability to oxidise environmental S‚Āį and thiosulphate, its clustered modules are spatially committed to internal handoff ‚ÄĒ external scavenging is less competitive than in Purus. In intermediate-rich fluid zones where Purus activity has accumulated significant S‚Āį and thiosulphate, Purus outcompetes Condensus by exploiting resources Condensus's architecture cannot access as efficiently.

#### Ecological Position
Condensus dominates clean sulphate-limited high density zones. It is outcompeted by Purus in intermediate-rich fluid where external scavenging efficiency matters more than internal cycling efficiency. The two lineages are chemically entangled ‚ÄĒ Purus generates the intermediates Condensus cannot scavenge efficiently, while Condensus's sulphate preservation partially benefits Purus by slowing the depletion of their shared sulphate pool. Neither lineage evolved toward this relationship deliberately, but both persist within it across the remainder of the Erebian Era.
