### Overview
[[Sulphovita Captor]] Carpens is the derived lineage of [[Sulphovita Captor]], emerging at approximately 250 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Captor Legens]] populations through progressive refinement of the cleavage mechanism and the emergence of engulfment capability. Where Legens scavenges passively and inefficiently, Carpens harvests actively and thoroughly — locating recently dead organisms during the pre-rupture window and engulfing them whole rather than waiting for membrane rupture to release fragments into the fluid. It is the most ecologically novel organism of the Erebian Era and the first life on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] to physically interact with another organism.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** High density Primalis zone boundaries within Pocket B, overlapping with and progressively displacing [[Sulphovita Captor Legens]] across all Captor-occupied zones
- **Temperature:** ~80–120°C, consistent with [[Sulphovita Captor]] range
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Captor]] range
- **Chemistry:** Consistent with [[Sulphovita Captor#Environmental Niche]]. Carpens is additionally capable of sustaining itself through brief periods of external substrate scarcity via engulfment resource pulses
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Environmental Niche]] entirely.

### Genetics
Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Genetics]] entirely, with the addition of engulfment machinery — a suite of peptide modules coordinating localised membrane lipid synthesis, directional extension, and membrane fusion around an external target. Represents the most structurally complex peptide module innovation of the Erebian Era.

### Metabolism
Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Metabolism]] entirely.

### Membrane
Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Membrane]] entirely, with the addition of engulfment capability. The sulphonolipid membrane develops the capacity to extend outward, surround an external target, and fuse around it — enclosing the target within a membrane-bounded internal engulfment vacuole for direct processing. See [[Sulphovita Captor#Engulfment Mechanism]].

### Reproduction
Follows [[Sulphovita#Reproduction]] entirely. Carpens replication rates exceed those of all other [[Sulphovita]] lineages in debris-rich zones — refined cleavage efficiency and engulfment resource pulses provide a monomer acquisition advantage that compounds across generations under high density conditions.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

A recently engulfed dead organism represents days to weeks of internal peptide material, buffering Carpens against short-term chemical fluctuation more effectively than any other [[Sulphovita]] lineage. This internal resource reserve extends Carpens's effective dormancy resistance in amino acid-scarce conditions — a successful engulfment shortly before substrate depletion can sustain Carpens through periods that would force other [[Sulphovita]] into dormancy.

### Ecological Character
Carpens outcompetes [[Sulphovita Captor Legens]] across all zones — superior cleavage efficiency provides advantage in passive scavenging contexts, engulfment provides advantage wherever recently dead organisms are present. Legens has no competitive counter to either innovation and is progressively displaced across all Captor-occupied zones, driven to extinction at approximately 100 Million AC.

Carpens coexists with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] lineages without direct competitive exclusion for chemical energy substrates. In high density mixed zones Carpens and Primalis partition resource acquisition between them — Primalis consuming abiotic chemical energy and free monomers, Carpens harvesting the biological debris Primalis populations generate. Carpens's presence progressively concentrates biological material within its own populations rather than returning it to the broader fluid environment, a dynamic with no precedent in Xenian biological history.

Engulfment of living [[Sulphovita]] is possible in principle but is not predation under Erebian Era conditions. Living organisms actively maintain membrane integrity — Carpens membrane extension against a healthy living target stalls against continuous membrane repair, consuming resources without successfully enclosing the target. The threshold at which engulfment rate exceeds repair rate is not reached under the stable chemical conditions of the Erebian Era.

This changes in the Photian Era. Environmental disruption and intensified resource competition will produce conditions where living targets are sufficiently stressed that membrane repair rates drop below Carpens's engulfment rate — the point at which Captor transitions from scavenger to predator.

### Evolution
[[Sulphovita Captor]] Carpens emerges at approximately 250 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Captor Legens]] populations through progressive refinement of the cleavage mechanism and the gradual emergence of engulfment capability. As with all major [[Sulphovita]] divergences there is no single event — engulfment capability accumulates incrementally across generations until it becomes ecologically significant.

Carpens subsequently displaces Legens across all zones, driving it to extinction at approximately 100 Million AC. It has no further divergence events within the Erebian Era, entering the Photian Era as the sole surviving Captor lineage — carrying engulfment machinery already capable of targeting living organisms under sufficiently stressful conditions, poised to transition from scavenger to predator as capture transforms Pocket B beyond anything the Erebian biosphere has encountered.
