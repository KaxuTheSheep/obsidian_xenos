### Overview
[[Sulphovita Captor]] Legens is the founding lineage of [[Sulphovita Captor]] and the first organism on [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]] to exploit biological debris as a resource. Emerging at approximately 450 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] populations at the monomer-scarce boundaries of high density Primalis zones, Legens develops a primitive surface-bound cleavage module capable of binding and partially processing fragmented peptide structures at the outer membrane surface — the first biological resource acquisition from another organism in Xenian history.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** High density Primalis zone boundaries within Pocket B, where fragmented peptide debris accumulates faster than it dissolves back into the free amino acid pool
- **Temperature:** ~80–120°C, consistent with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] zones
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] range
- **Chemistry:** Reduced free amino acid availability, abundant fragmented peptide debris from Primalis population die-off, otherwise chemically consistent with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] zones
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Sulphovita Captor#Environmental Niche]] entirely.

### Ecological Character
Legens outcompetes [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] in monomer-scarce debris-rich zones at population boundaries, replicating faster than Purus wherever fragment debris is available. In vent-proximal zones where free amino acid availability remains high, Purus's simpler architecture gives it a marginal efficiency advantage — Legens carries the overhead of cleavage module maintenance without proportional benefit where free monomers are abundant.

Legens is outcompeted by [[Sulphovita Captor Carpens]] across all zones once Carpens emerges — superior cleavage efficiency and engulfment capability provide advantages in both debris-rich and debris-scarce conditions that Legens cannot match. Legens is progressively displaced across all Captor-occupied zones, becoming the second extinct organism in Xenian biological history at approximately 100 Million AC.

### Evolution
[[Sulphovita Captor]] Legens emerges at approximately 450 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] populations already stressed by monomer scarcity at high density zone boundaries. The divergence was gradual — cleavage module efficiency increasing across generations until fragment scavenging contributed meaningfully to monomer acquisition. There was no single divergence event but a progressive shift in populations operating at the margins of free amino acid availability.

Legens gives rise to [[Sulphovita Captor Carpens]] at approximately 250 Million AC through progressive refinement of the cleavage mechanism and the emergence of engulfment capability. Carpens subsequently displaces Legens across all zones, driving it to extinction at approximately 100 Million AC.
