### Overview
[[Sulphovita Copula]] Incitor is the founding lineage of [[Sulphovita Copula]], emerging at approximately 500 Million AC from peripheral [[Sulphovita Primalis]] populations under chronic H₂S scarcity. Incitor is the opportunist stage of the Copula lineage — the first [[Sulphovita]] to possess iron-coordinated methane activation, but not yet fully committed to it. It represents the transitional form between baseline [[Sulphovita]] dual-pathway dependency and the complete metabolic specialisation of its descendant [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]].

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Intermediate peripheral zones of Pocket B, between vent-proximal [[Sulphovita Primalis]] territory and the most H₂S-depleted margins occupied by [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]]
- **Temperature:** ~50–80°C
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Copula]] range
- **Chemistry:** Fluctuating H₂S availability, moderate to high Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, methane abundant
- **Energy source:** Methane oxidation via either iron-coordinated activation or H₂S priming depending on local chemical availability

#### Dual Activation Strategy
Ferric coordinase is present and functional in Incitor but represents a relatively recent evolutionary innovation, not yet refined to the point where it can fully displace H₂S priming under all conditions. Incitor switches contextually between activation mechanisms based on local chemical availability:

- **High Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, low H₂S** — Ferric coordinase operates as primary activation mechanism, iron-coordinated methane oxidation dominant
- **High H₂S, low Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺** — H₂S priming operates as primary activation mechanism, baseline [[Sulphovita]] behaviour
- **High Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, high H₂S** — both mechanisms available, organism exploits whichever local conditions favour
- **Low Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, low H₂S** — metabolic activity reduced, organism enters low activity state

This opportunism is Incitor's defining character — not a specialist but an organism in transition, capable of exploiting chemical conditions that would strand baseline [[Sulphovita]] while retaining full baseline capability where H₂S is available.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita Copula#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

Incitor has the most resilient dormancy profile of any Copula lineage. Its dual-mechanism architecture means that if Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability drops below Ferric coordinase threshold, Incitor can partially sustain metabolic activity on H₂S priming while iron concentrations recover. Conversely if H₂S drops below priming threshold, iron-coordinated activation can sustain reduced metabolic activity independently. True full dormancy in Incitor requires both mechanisms to fail simultaneously — a less common scenario than single-substrate depletion. This metabolic flexibility extends Incitor's effective dormancy resistance beyond what either mechanism alone would provide.

### Ecological Character
Incitor occupies the intermediate zones of Pocket B where H₂S availability fluctuates sufficiently to make pathway flexibility valuable. It outcompetes baseline [[Sulphovita]] in iron-rich, H₂S-poor peripheral zones, and is outcompeted by baseline [[Sulphovita]] in H₂S-rich vent-proximal zones where the fixed overhead of iron retention is a liability rather than an asset — Incitor pays the cost of maintaining the iron retention module regardless of whether Ferric coordinase is actively running.

Incitor's intermediate niche proves ultimately untenable. As [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]] emerges and refines iron-coordinated activation to a degree that renders Incitor uncompetitive even in iron-rich zones, and baseline [[Sulphovita]] continues to dominate H₂S-rich zones, Incitor's dual-pathway flexibility becomes a metabolic liability rather than an asset. Its viable niche narrows progressively until population collapse becomes inevitable.

### Evolution
[[Sulphovita Copula]] Incitor is the founding Copula lineage — the first expression of iron-coordinated methane activation in Xenian biological history. It emerges gradually from peripheral Primalis populations rather than through any discrete divergence event, representing the earliest stage of a progressive shift away from H₂S dependency.

Incitor gives rise to [[Sulphovita Copula Solutus]] at approximately 320 Million AC as the most H₂S-depleted peripheral populations refine iron-coordinated activation to the point of complete H₂S oxidation abandonment. Solutus subsequently outcompetes Incitor across all zones, driving it to extinction at approximately 200 Million AC — the first extinction event in Xenian biological history.
