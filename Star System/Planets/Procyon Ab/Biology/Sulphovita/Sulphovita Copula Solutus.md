### Overview
Sulphovita Copula Solutus is the derived lineage of [[Sulphovita Copula]], emerging at approximately 320 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] populations in the most H₂S-depleted peripheral zones of Pocket B. Where Incitor retained H₂S oxidation as a fallback, Solutus has abandoned it entirely — the first organism on Xenos to completely relinquish a founding metabolic pathway. It is the most metabolically specialised organism of the Erebian Era and occupies the narrowest ecological niche of any Sulphovita lineage.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** Most extreme peripheral zones of Pocket B, furthest from active vent outflow and most H₂S-depleted
- **Temperature:** ~50–70°C
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Copula]] range
- **Chemistry:** Lowest H₂S availability of any Sulphovita zone, highest relative Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability within Pocket B, methane abundant
- **Energy source:** Methane oxidation via iron-coordinated activation exclusively. H₂S oxidation absent.

### Metabolism
Follows [[Sulphovita Copula#Metabolism]] entirely, with the exception that H₂S oxidation is non-functional. Solutus operates exclusively on iron-coordinated methane oxidation via Ferric coordinase. H₂S encountered in the environment is not metabolised — it passes through membrane transport systems without triggering oxidation pathways.

#### H₂S Oxidation Abandonment
Solutus does not lose H₂S oxidation through any active deletion mechanism — Sulphovita's peptide architecture has no genome from which modules can be removed. Instead, abandonment occurs through **distributional marginalisation**. As Ferric coordinase becomes increasingly refined across Incitor generations, iron-coordinated activation progressively dominates the internal peptide distribution. H₂S oxidation modules, no longer contributing meaningfully to energy acquisition, accumulate replication errors that go unselected against. Their representation in the peptide network falls across generations until they drop below the functional threshold required to sustain the pathway. The modules are not erased — they are crowded out, becoming vestigial remnants of insufficient concentration to catalyse meaningful chemistry.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita Copula#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

Solutus has the most precarious dormancy profile of any organism in the Erebian Era biosphere. With H₂S oxidation completely absent from its peptide network, Solutus has no fallback when Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺ availability drops below Ferric coordinase threshold. Dormancy is triggered immediately and exclusively by iron scarcity — there is no partial metabolic state, no alternative pathway to slow the decline. Once dormant Solutus degrades at the same rate as any Sulphovita, but enters dormancy more readily and with no recovery flexibility if iron availability does not return within the dormancy window.

Solutus transitions from full metabolic activity to irreversible collapse without intermediate states — unlike Primalis, which retains dual-pathway flexibility even in dormancy onset. Solutus population dynamics are characteristically volatile as a result — stable under favourable iron conditions, catastrophically vulnerable to any sustained iron depletion event.

### Ecological Character
Solutus dominates the most extreme peripheral zones of Pocket B where H₂S availability is lowest and iron availability is highest. Within that niche it is unrivalled — no other Sulphovita lineage can sustain methane oxidation under conditions this H₂S-depleted. It is outcompeted by [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] in intermediate zones where H₂S fluctuates sufficiently to make pathway flexibility valuable, and by baseline Sulphovita in vent-proximal zones where H₂S is abundant.

Solutus's volatility makes it an ecologically unstable presence in the peripheral zones it dominates. Population crashes driven by localised iron depletion — Copula organisms competing for the same iron pool with limited vent replenishment — are a recurring feature of Solutus population dynamics across the later Erebian Era. Recovery follows when iron concentrations stabilise, but the pattern of boom and collapse is a defining characteristic of the most specialised organism Xenos has yet produced.

This vulnerability will become critically significant during the Photian Era, when environmental upheaval disrupts the stable chemical gradients Solutus depends on entirely.

### Evolution
Sulphovita Copula Solutus emerges at approximately 320 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Copula Incitor]] populations in the most H₂S-depleted peripheral zones of Pocket B, as iron-coordinated activation becomes sufficiently refined to operate as the sole metabolic pathway. It subsequently outcompetes and drives Incitor to extinction at approximately 200 Million AC — the first extinction event in Xenian biological history — by rendering Incitor's dual-pathway flexibility a liability rather than an asset in every zone both lineages occupy.

Solutus has no further divergence events within the Erebian Era. It enters the Photian Era as the sole surviving Copula lineage, occupying the most precarious ecological position of any Sulphovita organism.
