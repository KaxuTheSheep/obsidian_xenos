### Overview
Sulphovita Primalis Condensus is the derived lineage of [[Sulphovita Primalis]], emerging from [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] populations at approximately 575 Million AC. It represents not a biochemical innovation but a spatial reorganisation of existing Primalis machinery — the same sulphur oxidation modules present in Purus, progressively tightened into functional clustering within the peptide network. Condensus is the first instance of regionalised biochemical identity within a single Sulphovita organism.

### Environmental Niche
- **Location:** High density vent-proximal zones of Pocket B within the broader [[Sulphovita Primalis]] range. Favours clean sulphate-limited zones where internal cycling efficiency outweighs external scavenging as the dominant selective pressure
- **Temperature:** ~80–120°C
- **Pressure:** High subsurface, consistent with [[Sulphovita Primalis]] range
- **Chemistry:** Highest H₂S and methane availability within Pocket B. Lower ambient S⁰ and thiosulphate concentrations relative to zones dominated by Purus activity
- **Energy source:** Follows [[Sulphovita Primalis#Environmental Niche]] entirely.

#### Sulphur Oxidation Clustering
Condensus's defining metabolic trait is the progressive tightening of sulphur oxidation module clustering within the peptide network. The same Sulphide oxidase, Sulphur oxidase, and Sulphite oxidase modules present in Purus are repositioned into sufficiently close proximity that intermediates — S⁰ and SO₃²⁻ — are handed off directly between modules before they can diffuse away. The result is a near-complete internal sulphur cycle, significantly reducing outward sulphur flux relative to Purus.

Three consequences follow from this clustering:

**Sulphate preservation** — reduced intermediate escape means less S⁰ and thiosulphate accumulating in surrounding fluid. In high density zones where multiple organisms compete for the same sulphate pool, Condensus degrades the shared chemical environment significantly less than Purus, compounding its competitive advantage over generations.

**Proto-division of labour** — the sulphur processing cluster becomes a distinct functional zone within the peptide network, operating semi-independently from surrounding metabolic machinery. Not yet a true division of labour but a structural precursor to it — the first regionalised biochemical identity within a single Sulphovita organism.

**Consistent daughter inheritance** — clustered modules replicate in close proximity, making it more likely that daughter organisms inherit the full functional cluster intact. Condensus daughters are more biochemically consistent than Purus daughters, partially counteracting the inherent asymmetry of [[Sulphovita]] reproduction. Population-level variation is reduced but reliable transmission of the clustering innovation is increased.

#### Limitations
Condensus's inward-oriented clustering makes it less efficient at processing external sulphur intermediates from surrounding fluid. Its clustered modules are spatially committed to internal handoff — external scavenging is less competitive than in Purus. In intermediate-rich zones where Purus activity has accumulated significant S⁰ and thiosulphate, Purus outcompetes Condensus by exploiting resources Condensus's architecture cannot access as efficiently.

### Dormancy and Death
Follows [[Sulphovita Primalis#Dormancy and Death]] for all universal mechanisms.

Condensus enters dormancy with a tighter, more densely clustered network. The sulphur processing cluster degrades as a unit rather than piecemeal — Condensus either retains functional sulphur cycling or loses it entirely, with fewer intermediate degradation states than Purus. Dormancy windows are marginally longer than Purus but recovery is more binary — either the cluster is intact enough to resume or it is not.

### Ecological Character
Condensus dominates clean sulphate-limited high density zones within Pocket B's vent-proximal regions. Its sulphate preservation makes it the less ecologically destructive lineage in shared chemical environments — where Purus continuously degrades the sulphate pool, Condensus partially maintains it, compounding its competitive advantage in zones where sulphate is the limiting resource.

Condensus is outcompeted by [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] in intermediate-rich fluid where external scavenging efficiency matters more than internal cycling efficiency. The two lineages are chemically entangled — Purus generates the intermediates Condensus cannot scavenge efficiently, while Condensus's sulphate preservation partially benefits Purus by slowing depletion of their shared sulphate pool. Neither lineage evolved toward this relationship deliberately, but both persist within it across the remainder of the Erebian Era.

### Evolution
Sulphovita Primalis Condensus emerges at approximately 575 Million AC from [[Sulphovita Primalis Purus]] populations under chronic high density sulphate limitation. The divergence is not a novel biochemical invention but a structural drift — populations in which replication errors happened to place sulphur oxidation modules in closer proximity gained a marginal efficiency advantage, and that advantage compounded across generations until clustering became the defining organisational trait of a distinct lineage.

Condensus has no further divergence events within the Erebian Era. It persists as a stable lineage — its clustering innovation sufficient to maintain competitive dominance in sulphate-limited zones without further architectural change.
