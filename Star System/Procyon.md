## Overview
The Procyon system sits approximately 11.5 light years from Earth, hosting two stars with a violent shared history — [[Procyon A\|Kairos]] and [[Procyon B\|Lethe]]. The system is otherwise empty; any planets that formed naturally around Kairos were almost certainly destroyed or ejected during [[Procyon B|Lethe]]'s red giant phase. [[Procyon Ab\|Xenos]] is the only world of significance, a captured rogue planet whose presence is both improbable and defining.

## Stars
[[Procyon A\|Kairos]] is an F-type orange subgiant — the primary energy source for [[Procyon Ab\|Xenos]], slowly exhausting its hydrogen fuel and beginning the long process of stellar evolution that its companion already underwent.

[[Procyon B\|Lethe]] is a white dwarf — the remnant of a star that once expanded into a red giant, engulfing Kairos in a common envelope event before collapsing into its current state. It now emits strongly in ultraviolet, making it a persistent radiation source across the system.

The two stars share a circumbinary orbit with [[Procyon Ab|Xenos]], their relative positions constantly shifting and creating complex, unpredictable illumination cycles across the planet's surface.

## Planets
[[Procyon Ab\|Xenos]] is the only planet in the system — a captured rogue that did not form here but was gravitationally bound to the system after drifting through interstellar space. Its presence defines everything about the Procyon system's present character.
