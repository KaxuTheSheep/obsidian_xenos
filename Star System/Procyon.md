# Procyon
## Overview
The Procyon system sits approximately 11.5 light years from Earth, hosting two stars with a violent shared history — Kairos and Lethe. The system is otherwise empty; any planets that formed naturally around Kairos were almost certainly destroyed or ejected during Lethe's red giant phase. Xenos is the only world of significance, a captured rogue planet whose presence is both improbable and defining.

## Stars
### Kairos (Procyon A)
An F5 IV-V subgiant — the primary star of the system and the dominant energy source for Xenos. Kairos is actively evolving off the main sequence, its hydrogen core nearly exhausted. It is significantly more luminous than the Sun and will eventually expand into a true subgiant and then a giant, fundamentally altering the system's energy environment on astronomical timescales. See [[Procyon A]] for full parameters.

### Lethe (Procyon B)
A DQZ white dwarf — the collapsed remnant of a star that once dominated the system. Lethe's progenitor underwent a red giant phase approximately 1.19 billion years ago, expanding into a common envelope with Kairos before shedding its outer layers and collapsing. The common envelope event reshaped the binary orbit and almost certainly destroyed or ejected any planets that had formed in the system. Lethe now emits strongly in ultraviolet but contributes negligible thermal energy relative to Kairos. See [[Procyon B]] for full parameters.

## Binary Orbit
Kairos and Lethe orbit their common barycentre with a period of **40.84 years** along an elliptical orbit with an eccentricity of **0.40** — more eccentric than Mercury's orbit around the Sun. Their separation varies considerably:

- **Average separation:** ~15 AU
- **Periastron:** ~8.9 AU
- **Apastron:** ~21 AU

This eccentricity means the gravitational influence of Lethe on the outer system varies significantly across each 40-year cycle, creating a periodically shifting gravitational landscape that any body in a wide orbit around the system must navigate.

## Planets
Xenos is the only planet in the system — a captured rogue that did not form here but was gravitationally bound through a three-body interaction during its passage through the system. Its capture is the defining event of the Procyon system's present character and the origin of all known complex chemistry and life in the system. See [[Procyon Ab]] for full documentation.
