# obsidian_xenos
A structured worldbuilding project documenting Xenos (Procyon Ab) — a captured rogue planet with an iron-dominated composition, extreme pressure atmosphere, and a subsurface ocean sustaining two independent origins of life.

## Overview
Xenos is a high-density rogue world that formed outside the Procyon system and was later captured through gravitational interaction. Its environment is defined by:

- A massive iron–nickel core driving intense geothermal activity
- A global subsurface ocean beneath a thick ice shell
- A dense primordial atmosphere (~47 atm)
- Continuous volcanism and hydrothermal vent systems

All known life originates in these vent systems, forming two fundamentally different biological radices:

- **Ferrovita** — mineral-based, iron-redox life inhabiting the deepest vent zones
- **Sulphovita** — peptide-based, sulphur-cycle life inhabiting the peripheral vent zones

## Structure
This repository is organised as an interconnected Obsidian knowledge base:

- **Core Planet**
  - `Procyon Ab (Xenos)` — central planetary overview
- **Environment**
  - `Atmosphere` — composition and evolution
  - `Geology` — crust, core, volcanism, terrain
  - `Hydrology` — subsurface ocean and chemical environments
- **Biology**
  - `Ferrovita` — iron-based radix, domains Firmus and Novus
  - `Sulphovita` — peptide-based radix, domains Primalis, Copula, and Captor
- **Systems**
  - `Timeline` — planetary and biological history
  - `Glossary` — standardised definitions and eras

## Key Concepts

- **Rogue Origin** — Xenos formed in a different star system and was later captured into the Procyon binary
- **Subsurface Biosphere** — all life exists entirely beneath a 17 km ice shell prior to stellar capture
- **Dual Abiogenesis** — two independent origins of life arising in separate, chemically incompatible environments
- **Chemically Driven Evolution** — biological development tightly coupled to geochemical gradients with no photosynthetic pathway

## Status
Active development. Core planetary systems and both biological radices are established through the Erebian Era, including full domain and lineage documentation. Further expansion will focus on:

- Photian Era events and post-capture environmental transformation
- Interaction and competition between biological radices
- Ecosystem complexity and diversification under stellar radiation

Xenos is not a habitable world in the conventional sense — it is a chemically active system where life emerges from geology rather than existing alongside it.
