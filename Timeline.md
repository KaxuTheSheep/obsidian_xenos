  + Formation of Xenos

+ Erebian Era
+

+ ~1 Billion AC
  + Ejection of Xenos
    +Gravitational instability within its birth system ultimately ejects Xenos from its original orbit. During this chaotic phase, the planet undergoes repeated collisions with asteroid belt material, accreting significant quantities of water and mineral matter. As it is accelerated out of the system at velocities approaching ~150 km/s, surface temperatures rapidly decline. Any exposed water freezes, initiating the formation of a global ice shell.

Once fully ejected into interstellar space, Xenos enters a prolonged rogue phase. The surface remains entirely frozen, sealed beneath an ice layer that eventually stabilises at approximately 17 km thickness. Beneath this shell, however, geothermal heat generated by the planet’s massive iron-rich interior sustains a global subsurface liquid ocean. Above, a dense primordial atmosphere persists at ~47 atm, dominated by hydrogen and helium, largely retained due to the planet’s strong gravity and lack of stellar stripping.
+ ~800 Million AC
  + Emergence of Life
    + First prokaryotic life emerges in deep hydrothermal vent systems within two vent clusters. [[Ferrovita]] and [[Sulphovita]] arise as completely independent origins of life in chemically distinct zones of the same vent environment. No interaction between domains.

+
+ Photian Era
+
+ 0 AC / 0 PC
  + Capture of Xenos
    + Xenos is gravitationally captured into the Procyon system through a three-body interaction, formally becoming Procyon Ab. Light arrives on Xenos for the first time. The Photian Era begins.
